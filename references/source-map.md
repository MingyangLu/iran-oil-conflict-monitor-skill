# Source Map

Use these source groups in roughly this order.

## 1. Official / Primary Sources

### United States

- White House: statements, remarks, transcripts
- Truth Social official Trump account pages when accessible
- US Treasury: sanctions, OFAC designations
- US Department of State: press statements and briefings
- US Department of Defense / CENTCOM: military announcements when relevant

### Iran

- khamenei.ir: supreme leader statements
- president.ir: president statements
- mfa.gov.ir: foreign ministry positions
- IRNA: state news agency, useful for official framing

## 2. Shipping / Energy System Sources

- Major shipping intelligence providers if available in the environment
- Public market reporting summarizing tanker movement, delays, premiums, or war-risk insurance
- US EIA chokepoint background for baseline context, not for live counts

Use caution with:

- Social posts that count ships from AIS screenshots
- Unsourced “Hormuz closed” claims
- Old chokepoint statistics presented as live data

## 3. Market-Relevant Confirmation Sources

Use major wire services and established financial outlets to connect the event to prices:

- Reuters
- AP
- Bloomberg
- Financial Times
- Wall Street Journal

Prefer sources that include:

- Exact time of event
- Named officials
- Concrete shipping, sanctions, or military facts
- Reported Brent / WTI reaction

## Trump Post Source Priority

Use this order when the request involves Trump posts, “推文”, or social-media comments.

1. Truth Social original post from Trump's official account
2. Official site reproductions or embeds
3. Reuters / AP / Bloomberg / WSJ quoting the post with timestamp
4. X only if the post is clearly from Trump's official account and appears to be original or explicitly cross-posted

Be careful with:

- screenshots with no source URL
- aggregator accounts on X
- partial quotes with no timestamp
- articles that say “Trump said online” without specifying the platform

## 4. Price Context

When possible, verify:

- Front-month Brent direction
- Front-month WTI direction
- Whether the move is immediate, sustained, or reversed

If exact live prices are unavailable, describe directionality and explain the limitation.

## Impact Scoring Rubric

Classify each development on two axes.

### Probability of real barrel disruption

- Low: rhetoric with no operational change
- Medium: threats, deployments, inspections, or insurer warnings
- High: confirmed export loss, seizures, rerouting, closure, or sanctions bite

### Likely price effect

- Mild: short-lived headline reaction
- Moderate: multi-session risk premium or clear price response
- Strong: material disruption to supply or Strait transit

## Morning Note Judgment Guide

Use this quick mapping when filling the fixed template.

### Prefer `强利多`

- Confirmed export loss or production disruption
- Confirmed tanker seizures, rerouting, or meaningful Hormuz congestion
- New US or allied sanctions with immediate barrel impact

### Prefer `偏利多`

- Credible threat with some operational corroboration
- Military deployment or hardline rhetoric that raises shipping and insurance risk
- Market price action confirms risk premium is being rebuilt

### Prefer `中性`

- Conflicting signals
- Speeches without confirmed operational impact
- Dramatic headlines but stable transit and stable export evidence

### Prefer `偏利空`

- Clear easing signals from both sides
- Tanker flow normalizes after prior disruption concerns
- Oil initially rises on headlines but retraces as disruption fears fade

### Prefer `强利空`

- Verified de-escalation plus restoration of disrupted flow
- Sanctions relief or diplomatic breakthrough with credible supply upside
- Market reaction confirms risk premium is being unwound sharply

## Default Response Pattern

1. List the most important verified developments from the last 24 to 72 hours.
2. Separate official statements from third-party interpretation.
3. Explain the oil channel: supply, transit, sanctions, or sentiment.
4. State whether the effect is bullish, bearish, or mainly noise.
5. End with confidence and unresolved unknowns.
